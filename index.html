About

My path into development has been intentional. My background in Information System Security trained me to think in systems and structure. Psychology shaped how I think about people — how they interact, how they learn, and how they experience technology. Frontend development is where those two perspectives meet.

I’m confident in HTML and CSS, and I’m actively deepening my understanding of JavaScript. I focus on building strong fundamentals — variables, conditionals, state, debugging — because I care about understanding how things work, not just making them work. When I encounter something I don’t understand, I slow down, break it apart, and learn from it. That steady approach matters to me.

I’m also the founder of CodeHerWay, a platform where I document my journey into tech and create beginner-friendly resources for women who want to enter the field but feel intimidated by it. I believe development becomes empowering once you understand it — and I want to help make that shift easier for others.

Right now, I’m focused on contributing as a frontend developer in a growth-oriented environment, continuing to strengthen my JavaScript expertise, and expanding into React and UI/UX over time.

I’m building my career deliberately — with discipline, curiosity, and purpose.
